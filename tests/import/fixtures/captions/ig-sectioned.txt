Birria Tacos at home 🌮 better than the truck, I said what I said

For the birria:
3 lbs chuck roast, cut into large chunks
4 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
2 dried ancho chiles
3 chiles de arbol
1 white onion, quartered
6 garlic cloves
1 tbsp Mexican oregano
2 tsp cumin
1 cinnamon stick
4 cups beef stock
2 bay leaves

For the tacos:
Corn tortillas
2 cups Oaxaca cheese, shredded
White onion, finely diced
Fresh cilantro
Lime wedges

Toast the chiles in a dry pan 30 seconds per side. Soak them in hot water 15 minutes. Blend the chiles with onion, garlic, spices and 1 cup of stock until smooth. Sear the beef on all sides in a heavy pot. Pour the chile sauce over, add remaining stock and bay leaves. Braise covered at 300F for 3 hours until shreddable. Shred the beef and reserve the consommé. Dip tortillas in the fat layer of the consommé, fill with beef and cheese, and griddle until crispy. Serve with onion, cilantro, lime and a cup of consommé for dipping.
